Sneakily compelling ...
As soon as anyone starts say something thoughtful about Heizer or the social value of art, Pray jumps to another neat-looking wide shot of the rock to keep us oohing and aahing.
"Levitated Mass" is document, critique and celebration all in one.
Levitated Mass is more entertaining than a glowing artist profile has any right to be, because Pray remembers where his subject came from.
As a chronicle of a city falling in love with a rock, Levitated Mass shines. As a documentary on the nature of art, however, it misses a few big opportunities to reach for something truly fascinating and profound.
Pray's film, though not as weighty or groundbreaking - no pun intended - as its subject, nevertheless does a good job of presenting both art and artist ...
